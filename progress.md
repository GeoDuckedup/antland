- Construction projects now unlock biologically plausible descendants: the two upper deep galleries are available first, completing both unlocks the lower shaft, and completing that shaft unlocks its two forks. Incomplete faces use an oriented dark soil cap and irregular rim; dust motes only appear while real ants are digging.
- Excavation labor is capped at roughly 6.5% of the stable colony (8–14 workers). Workers remain committed through travel, digging, outward hauling, and deposition, preventing assignment inflation during long haul cycles.

- Phase 3 added a persistent queen and visible nursery brood arranged around her in distinct egg, larva, and pupa forms.
- Brood development is continuous and care-sensitive: larvae consume stored food, nurse presence changes development rate, pupae eclose as genuine persistent callow workers, and the queen lays only when food and nursery capacity allow.
- Food deliveries now build a colony reserve consumed by workers, larvae, and egg production. Low reserves suppress egg laying and reduce excavation allocation in favor of foraging.
- Worker allocation now responds to open construction fronts, brood pressure, and food pressure. Young/callow workers are favored for nursing; excavation tendencies and energy favor digging; the remainder forage.
- Workers now have explicit maturity stages and minor/media/major worker castes, reflected in body size, field notes, and deterministic state output.
- Phase 4 added a four-season environmental cycle. Each season changes food appearance rates, source mix, rain pressure, colony metabolism, surface color, reproduction, and construction priorities.
- Food sources now carry distinct nutritional values. Delivered fragments add reserves according to food quality, while seasonal scarcity changes what appears and how much is available.
- Added real worker injury, infection, recovery, senescence, starvation, death, persistent surface remains, and removal from the active labor pool. Queen reproduction and worker replacement now respond to losses and environmental pressure.
- Added a visible hunting beetle that pursues real surface workers. Nearby ants flee, attacks cause persistent injury or death, and successful hunters pause to consume competing food sources. `P` introduces a predator for observation.
- Disease outbreaks now begin, spread among underground workers, reduce health, and resolve through recovery or death. Infected and injured workers are visually tinted and exposed in field notes/state output.
- Colony priorities now switch between growth, food crisis, outbreak response, predator alarm, and winter conservation. Survival pressure suppresses laying and sharply reduces excavation in favor of foraging and nursing.
- Phase 5 added a fully autonomous rival colony with a separate surface nest, visually distinct slate workers, queen, food reserve, brood stages, worker eclosion, mortality, and demand-maintained forager/scout/guard roles.
- Both colonies now forage from the same food array and maintain separate pheromone fields. Rival workers evaluate source nutrition, establish their own return trails, visibly carry fragments home, and can exhaust food before the original colony reaches it.
- Territorial encounters are local and persistent rather than scripted. Workers defend, retreat according to condition and role, exchange caste-weighted damage, leave remains, and alter the frontier as food routes move. Per-worker combat recovery prevents swarm focus-fire.
- Added demand-driven sanitation workers. They locate surface remains, lift and carry them to a refuse midden, complete disposal trips, and delay future disease outbreaks.
- Rival guards remain committed until death; healthy foragers are promoted only when guard or scout counts fall below colony demand. This preserves territorial defense without making injured workers unrealistically change castes mid-fight.
- Phase 6 added heritable worker genomes for speed, size, disease resistance, aggression, and foraging acuity. Both queens have distinct founding genomes; every brood item carries a mutated inheritance into its persistent adult worker.
- Genetic traits now affect real mechanics: movement, body scale, initial health, infection progression, pheromone sensitivity, food evaluation, and caste-weighted combat. Living population averages and generation counts expose selection over time.
- Added a second predator strategy: a procedural web spider establishes a visible trap near the territorial frontier, slows workers inside the web, hunts only within the trap radius, and records casualties separately for each colony. `O` introduces it; `P` still introduces the roaming beetle.
- Seasonal landscape transformation now includes dynamic vegetation tint plus spring growth, autumn litter, and winter frost scatter layered across the ground. Winter still pauses both queens and reduces excavation.
- Added a full rival underground scan with four slate tunnel/chamber branches, a visible rival queen, and live brood-stage visualization. `N` switches underground focus between the amber home nest and slate rival nest.
